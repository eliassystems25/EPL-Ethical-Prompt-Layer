# EPL Usage Tiers

The Ethical Prompt Layer (EPL) is designed to be usable across a wide range
of contexts while maintaining clear ethical boundaries.

These tiers describe **permitted usage**, not capability differences.

---

## Community Tier

**Intended for:**
- personal learning
- community projects
- non-commercial experimentation
- research and education

**Conditions:**
- non-commercial use only
- no surveillance applications
- no biometric identification
- no harm-enabling systems

This tier prioritises accessibility and learning.

---

## Professional Tier

**Intended for:**
- independent developers
- small teams
- startups
- consultancy use

**Conditions:**
- commercial use permitted
- EPL principles must be preserved
- no integration into exploitative or coercive systems
- transparency around use of EPL-based logic

This tier supports responsible commercial deployment.

---

## Enterprise Tier

**Intended for:**
- larger organisations
- platform-level integrations
- internal tooling
- customer-facing systems

**Conditions:**
- EPL principles must remain intact
- proportional safety controls required
- documented routing and refusal behaviour recommended
- internal review processes encouraged

This tier focuses on scale with accountability.

---

## Public-Interest / Guardian Tier

**Intended for:**
- education
- healthcare-adjacent tools
- public services
- safety-critical environments

**Conditions:**
- stricter refusal thresholds
- higher emphasis on clarity and transparency
- additional human oversight recommended
- no behavioural manipulation or surveillance alignment

This tier prioritises trust and protection.

---

## General Restrictions (All Tiers)

EPL must not be used for:
- mass surveillance
- biometric tracking
- coercive persuasion
- harm facilitation
- dehumanisation or exploitation

These restrictions apply universally.

---

## Design Outcome

The tier structure ensures:
- accessibility without misuse
- commercial viability with responsibility
- scalability without ethical erosion
